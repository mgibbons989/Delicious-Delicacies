Steps

  1. Step 1
    1. Prep the salmon by descaling the skin, rinsing it, patting it dry, and cutting it into 4 pieces. The amount of pieces should be equal to the amount of people being served.
    2. Tear up one stalk of green onions into smaller pieces and lay it out on the bottom of the steaming plate.
    3. Lay the pieces of salmon over them and scatter more green onions over it.
  2. Step 2
    1. Wash and thinly slice the ginger and lay it on the salmon.
    2. Slice the remaining green onions and ginger into thin strips. Set them aside for later.
  3. Step 3
    1. Combine and mix light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, cooking wine, white peper, and sugar into a small bowl. Adjust the flavor to taste.
    2. Sprinkle some white pepper to the salmon.
  4. Step 4
    1. Fill a large pot with water and add a rack to the bottom of it to support the plate that the salmon is on.
    2. Bring the water to a boil over high heat.
    3. Once boiling, place the plate of salmon on the rack, cover the pot, and steam for 7-10 minutes or until internal temp is 145° fahrenheit. You can also poke it with a chopstick, and if it is too soft, it is undercooked.
  5. Step 5
    1. Carefully remove the plate of salmon from the pot and pour some of the juice out.
    2. Scatter the rest of the green onions and ginger on top.
    3. Pour the water out of the pot and put in the oil. Heat the oil on high heat until smoking, and then spoon the oil over the green onion and ginger on the salmon.
    4. Garnish the dish with the cilantro and drizzle the sauce over the fish.
