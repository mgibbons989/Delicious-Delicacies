Steps

  1. Step 1
    1. Combine the milk, flour, cornstarch, matcha powder, sguar, eggs, and melted butter into a blender and blend till smooth.
    2. Pour the batter through a sieve into a large bowl to make sure all the clumps are removed.
    3. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour or overnight.
  2. Step 2
    1. Heat some oil on a 8-10 inch pan over medium-low heat. The pan's size is how large the cake will be.
    2. Wipe off the excess oil and pour 1/4 cup of batter into the pan.
    3. Before it sets, swirl the batter around to coat the entire pan and cook for 2 minutes or until the texture turns matte.
    4. Flip and cook for another 15 seconds.
    5. Repeat this step until there is no more batter.
  3. Step 3 (Skip if you have whipping cream)
    1. Mix and beat the heavy whipping cream and sugar together in a stand mixer until stiff peaks.
    2. Place in the refrigerator until use.
  4. Step 4
    1. Roughly chop the chocolate into smaller pieces and sift the matcha powder over it.
    2. Heat the heavy whipping cream in a pot until steaming.
    3. Pour the cream over the chocolate and cover with a lid. Allow the chocolate to melt under the heat of the cream and stir until smooth. Set aside to cool while we move to step 5.
  5. Step 5
    1. Build the cake on a large plate by stacking crepe and a thin layer of whipping cream on top.
    2. Continue to build the cake layer by layer until there is no more crepes. You may stop at any time if you feel the cake is too tall.
    3. Top the cake with the ganache from step 4.
    4. Refrigerate for 1 hour before consuming.
  6. Notes
    • Fruits may be added on top or inside the layers if desired.
    • Store bought whipped cream is fine, but isn't as fresh.
    • Matcha powder can be removed and a simple crepe cake can still be made.
    • Feel free to add more matcha powder on top for appearance.
